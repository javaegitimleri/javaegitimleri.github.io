Ballistic Simulator

This page requires a Java aware browser.

The red vector on the moving ball is the velocity vector of the ball; green vectors are the horizontal and vertical components of the velocity vector.

Motion of the ball is also shown simultaneously on the coordinate axes (conventionally horizontal one is called x-axis, and vertical one is called y-axis) by regular dots;  these dots are blue when the ball moves in the positive direction, and red when it moves in the negative direction.

Your assignment is to hit the target under a variety of different conditions.

You can adjust the following parameters:

  • muzzle angle
  • muzzle velocity
  • gravitational field strength
  • wind speed

This virtual experiment is designed to measure the relation between muzzle velocity (which determines projectile energy), gravitational potential and the effects of frictional drag caused by wind.

Now try and hit the target.
